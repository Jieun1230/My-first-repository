My Favorite Taylor Swift Songs from Speak Now Album

This page shows you Jieun's favorite Taylor Swift Songs from Speak Now Album

Song #1 Enchanted


purple concert by Pixabay

“Enchanted” is a pop song on Taylor Swift’s 2010 album Speak Now. It describes meeting someone new and feeling instant wonder. She goes home and cannot stop thinking about that short moment. The guitars sparkle, and the sound slowly grows bigger and brighter. The bridge lifts the emotion and makes the hope feel urgent. Swift uses fairytale words to make an ordinary meeting feel magical. She worries that the feeling might be one-sided, but she still dreams. In the ending, she repeats a plea that the person is not with someone else. Overall, the song captures the first rush of infatuation and the wish for a real connection.

Song #2 Speak Now


bridemaids by Pixabay

“Speak Now” is the title track of Taylor Swift’s 2010 album Speak Now. The song tells the story of a girl who crashes a wedding to stop the man she loves from marrying someone else. It’s written like a romantic movie scene, full of tension and excitement. Swift sings from the perspective of someone who finally decides to speak up instead of staying silent. The melody is upbeat and playful, showing both fear and courage at the same time. Together with “Enchanted,” it shows how the album explores moments of honesty, emotion, and the power of saying what you truly feel.

Song #3 Mean


country guitar by Pixabay

“Mean” is another song from Taylor Swift’s 2010 album Speak Now. It talks about how people can be cruel and try to tear others down with their words. Swift wrote it as a response to those who criticized her harshly, turning their insults into motivation. The song mixes a country sound with strong, confident lyrics that show growth and self-respect. She promises that one day she will rise above all the negativity and be successful while the bullies stay the same. Like “Speak Now” and “Enchanted,” it shows a different side of her — brave, honest, and determined to speak her truth.

Song #4 Back to December


candle light by Pixabay

“Back to December” is a song from Taylor Swift’s 2010 album Speak Now. It is a sincere apology to someone she hurt in the past. She looks back on a winter romance and wishes she could fix her mistakes. The lyrics show regret, responsibility, and respect for the other person. Soft strings and piano create a calm, snowy mood. The chorus repeats the wish to “go back to December” and make things right. It feels more mature than many breakup songs because she owns her part in the pain. Overall, it’s a quiet, heartfelt letter set to music.

Song #5 Sparks Fly


electric guitar by Pixabay

“Sparks Fly” is a high-energy song on Taylor Swift’s 2010 album Speak Now. It describes instant chemistry that feels risky but exciting. She knows the person might be trouble, but she cannot resist. Electric guitars and steady drums keep the momentum strong. The chorus repeats “drop everything now,” which shows urgency and desire. Images of rain, sparks, and a crowded room make the scenes vivid. It balances thrill and doubt as she moves toward the choice. Overall, it’s about chasing a powerful feeling even if it might burn.
